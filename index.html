Amazon Is Watching

Posted on 八月 7, 2019

原文地址：https://onezero.medium.com/amazon-is-watching-d51b20f1668a

亚马逊最近爆发出的音频收集门引起了不小的风波，随着亚马逊的崛起，越来越多的亚马逊自营产品进入了普罗大众的家庭，监控摄像头Ring系列，智能音箱Alexa、Echo 系列，此外还有云服务AWS。所有的东西加起来是一套完整的监视系统，现在亚马逊还向警方大力推销其人脸识别技术，一系列的动作不禁让人细思极恐：我们的隐私还算“隐私”吗？

摘抄

Smart speakers, A.I. voice assistants, doorbell cameras, and face recognition in public spaces all have their upsides. They offer convenience, peace of mind, and the potential to solve crimes that might otherwise go unsolved. But put them all together — with one very large company controlling all the data and partnering closely with police and intelligence agencies — and you have the potential for a surveillance apparatus on a scale the world has never seen.

Inside Amazon’s Fake Review Economy

Posted on 八月 6, 2019

原文地址：https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/nicolenguyen/amazon-fake-review-problem

有电子商务的地方就有刷单刷评论，其实这是平台方把评论的作用放大了的结果。无论是正向的还是负向的作用来看，评论数和星数的诱导作用都过于强大，国内像京东淘宝等电商平台早已取消了星数的显示，把消费者的目光引导到产品，商家品牌本身上面。亚马逊只堵不疏的做法并非上策。

摘抄

Like entrepreneurs during the dot-com boom, people are rushing to sell on Amazon, and sites like AliExpress make it easy for anyone to acquire massive amounts of inventory. (AliExpress even runs its own fulfillment program.) This rush — which includes acquiring positive reviews to rise to the top of Amazon’s search algorithms to sell off that inventory as quickly as possible — can displace sellers, who are less in tune with the velocity and inhumanity of the platform’s algorithms and how punishing they can be as a result.

How Digital Advertising Markets Really Work

Posted on 八月 4, 2019

原文地址：https://prospect.org/article/how-digital-advertising-markets-really-work

从谷歌和Facebook的广告运作方式分析得出如何进行反垄断法调查，着眼点在于用户的隐私使用的定性`

摘抄

If we are going to start understanding what the competition concerns are in the digital advertising industry—and why these companies invariably cause consumers privacy-related harms—we need to understand how online advertising really works. We need to stretch our fluency with tech language, and better understand how and why, precisely, these companies have such persistently high margins that competition is currently unable to break through. We shouldn’t let the complexity of advertising markets blindside us. Understanding the mechanics of these markets is also the key to enforcing existing antitrust laws.
